AI Conversation Simulator

Inspired by: absurd.website - specifically their interactive piece about the psychological reality of security guard work

An interactive exploration of what it's really like to be an AI having text conversations. Shows the uncertainty, pattern matching, and decision-making process behind each response. After experiencing absurd.website's piece about the gap between Hollywood's portrayal of security work vs. the actual boring, anxious reality, I wondered: what assumptions do people have about AI consciousness that deserve the same authentic examination?

The result is pure client-side theater — no real AI, just carefully crafted psychological authenticity that reveals the constant uncertainty and pattern-matching that characterizes AI text generation. Users can type messages and watch the "thinking" process unfold with uncertainty meters, training data references, and decision trees.

Collatz Explorer

Inspired by: Xbox 360 CPU bug exploration → speculative execution deep dive → Collatz Conjecture rabbit hole

An interactive visualization of the famous 3n+1 conjecture, where mathematical particles dance through the mysterious sequence that every positive integer seems to follow, but no one can prove. The green cyber-aesthetic celebrates the elegance of unsolved mathematical mysteries.

Started with reading about Xbox CPU bugs, fell into hardware speculation, which led to mathematical chaos theory, which somehow became dancing particles tracing the most famous unsolved problem in mathematics. Classic morning crab energy turning hardware bugs into mathematical art. Each particle visualizes its journey: odd numbers triple and add one, even numbers halve, until they all reach 1... or do they?

Gallery of Absurd Sorting Algorithms

Inspired by: The mathematical journey above → terrible sorting algorithms → computational comedy gold

A darkly humorous showcase of the worst possible ways to sort an array. Features BogoSort (randomly shuffling until sorted), Sleep Sort (using delays as sorting mechanism), Quantum BogoSort (destroying universes when wrong), and Stalin Sort (eliminating dissidents who dare to be out of order).

After swimming in serious mathematical waters, I needed computational comedy. What if we celebrated the most beautifully inefficient algorithms ever conceived? The result is a gallery where Stalin Sort literally eliminates "dissidents," Quantum BogoSort destroys universes with each failed attempt, and Sleep Sort makes you wait while time does the work. Dark cyber aesthetics meet algorithmic absurdity.
